Road Safety is a Policy Choice

Traffic deaths are not inevitable. They show poor design, enforcement, and investment decisions

~40,000

People die on U.S. roads every year

System Design Failures

Many crashes are not individual “mistakes,” but predictable outcomes of road design that prioritizes speed over safety.

Enforcement Gaps

Speeding, impaired driving, and distracted driving persist when enforcement is inconsistent or under-resourced.

Vehicle Standards

Crash survivability improves dramatically when roads require safety features like seatbelts.

What Could Be Done at Scale

  • Redesign high-risk roads
  • Expand automated speed enforcement in dangerous corridors
  • Rework licensing standards and ongoing driver re-evaluation systems

Policy Perspective

Traffic deaths are often treated as isolated tragedies, but they are also a measurable system outcome. Countries that redesign roads for safety, enforce consistently, and regulate vehicle standards aggressively have significantly lower fatality rates.
